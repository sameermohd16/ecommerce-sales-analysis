E-Commerce Sales Analysis Using SQL

📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes an e-commerce sales dataset using SQL to extract meaningful business insights. The objective is to explore the database, answer real-world business questions, and provide data-driven recommendations based on customer, product, and order information.

This project demonstrates practical SQL skills used in data analysis, including data exploration, aggregation, joins, filtering, and business reporting.

---

🎯 Project Objectives

- Understand the database structure and table relationships.
- Explore customer, product, order, and category data.
- Solve business problems using SQL queries.
- Generate meaningful business insights from query results.
- Practice real-world data analysis using SQL.

---

📂 Dataset

The project contains five related tables:

| Table | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Customers | Stores customer information including name, city, and state. |
| Orders | Stores customer order details, order dates, and order status. |
| Order Items | Stores products purchased in each order and their quantities. |
| Products | Stores product information including category and price. |
| Categories | Stores product category details. |

- Aggregate Functions (COUNT, SUM, AVG, MAX, MIN)

📊 Business Requirements

The analysis answers the following business questions:

- Prepare an overall business summary.
- Analyze monthly order trends.
- Analyze the distribution of order statuses.
- Track monthly cancelled orders.
- Identify states with the highest number of customers.
- Identify cities with the highest number of customers.
- Identify the most loyal customers.
- Identify one-time customers.
- Identify the top-selling products.
- Identify slow-moving products.
- Analyze products by category.
- Analyze category sales performance.
- Identify orders with the highest number of different products.
- Identify orders with the highest quantity purchased.
- Calculate the average number of products per order.
- Calculate the average units purchased per order.
- Identify premium-priced products.
- Identify the lowest-priced products.
- Compare the average product price by category.
- Generate a product performance report.

---

📈 Key Findings

- The business has an active customer base with 100 customers and 500 orders.
- Approximately 90% of orders were successfully delivered.
- Customer orders increased steadily until March and remained relatively stable afterward.
- Customer distribution is concentrated in a few states and cities.
- Cookware Set is the highest-selling product based on units sold.
- Electronics and Sports categories recorded the highest sales volume.
- Customers purchase an average of 2.39 different products and 4.98 units per order.
- The product catalog contains both premium and budget-friendly products.

---

💡 Business Recommendations

- Maintain sufficient inventory for high-demand products.
- Monitor slow-moving products and review their performance regularly.
- Strengthen customer loyalty programs for repeat customers.
- Increase marketing efforts in regions with lower customer concentration.
- Continue monitoring monthly order and cancellation trends.
- Review category performance to improve product assortment.
